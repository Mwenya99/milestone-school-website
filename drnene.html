School Profile 2023 - 2024

Milestone School is a mixed Nursery and Primary school located in Luangwa. The school is built on the vision of creating a strong and sustainable center of learning that offers affordable and high quality education to the community. The school opened in January 2008 and now has more than thirty-five members of staff. The teaching staff is highly qualified and experienced.

General Contact: +260 979 867 508

Our Vision

A community where every child can access quality education for sustainable development.

Our Mission

We aim to support learners in enjoying their right to education by providing comprehensive Nursery and Primary learning in a safe and secure environment.

Accreditation

The school is fully registered with the Ministry of Education and is also an approved examination center by the Examination Council of Zambia.

Where We Want to Be

A second campus is under construction and is expected to be complete by December 2024.

Programs and Extra-Curricular

We provide strong literacy and numeracy programs along with science and student wellbeing. Staff take part in continued professional growth and the school uses clear assessment and reporting. Students enjoy sports, art, media, STEAM, soccer, volleyball, netball, and ICT.

Enrolment

The school began in 2008 with only six nursery children. It now offers learning from Nursery to Primary Seven and supports more than four hundred learners.

Director Profile

Miranda Nene: The Mind Behind Milestone School

Miranda Mutinta Nene is an accomplished educator with more than a decade of experience. She holds a Bachelor of Education and a Master of Education from Mulungushi University. Over sixteen years, she has managed Milestone School and worked with several organizations, including serving as a teacher with the Ministry of Education.

Her skills include team leadership, attention to detail, problem-solving, and strong communication. She has guided the school in community efforts such as support to Kakama and Buyantashi Primary Schools. The school has awarded bursaries to learners and has helped students in universities and secondary schools.

Miranda also served as chairperson for the Kabwe Chapter of Ladies Circle Zambia, participating in humanitarian programs.
